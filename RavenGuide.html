Part 1: Introduction

Raven’s Identity and Archetype

Raven in Tekken 8 Season 2 is a technical mid-range mixup specialist, combining clone threats, teleport mobility, and burst offense. He controls neutral space through safe mids, counter hit tools, and calculated clone summon traps.

Playstyle Overview

  • Mid-Range Dominance: Long pokes and clone setups to control neutral.
  • Pressure Layers: Fake pressure into real traps using Shadow Sprint and Soulzone cancels.
  • Defensive Trickery: Evade punishment with teleports and clone fakeouts.
  • High Risk / High Reward: Demands precision in setups and movement control.

Core Strengths

  • Exceptional spacing tools and safe mid pokes
  • Explosive damage off successful clone mindgames
  • Versatile defensive resets via teleport and clones
  • Elite tempo disruption ability

Core Weaknesses

  • Requires constant variation — cannot autopilot
  • Back Turn stance is vulnerable if misused
  • Clone setups can be baited if obvious
  • Weaker static defense compared to top tiers

Mindset Recommendation

Think of Raven not just as a character, but as a tempo disruptor. Win fights by confusing, trapping, and forcing mistakes — not by simply out-damaging.

Part 6: Gameplan

Overall Strategy

Raven wins by **disrupting timing, movement, and mental flow** — not by raw frame traps. The goal is to overload the opponent’s reaction stack faster than they can stabilize.

Neutral Control Core

  • 1 / 1,1: Safe fast pokes for checks
  • D/F+1: Safe mid poke into mind trap
  • B+2: Long range poke denial
  • WR 2: Plus frames at distance

Approach Systems

  • F,F+3: Sprint Knee Approach
  • QCF+1: Dash Pressure Jab
  • Clone Fake Dash: Mind Overload Setup

Flow Disruption Layers

  • Neutral ➔ Poke ➔ Sidestep ➔ Fake Pressure ➔ Clone ➔ Teleport ➔ Reset

Mindset Priority

  • Disrupt Timing: Never repeat same flow twice
  • Force Panic: Create frames they don't expect
  • Reset Rhythm: Teleport and Clone mind resets constantly

Elite Raven players do not let opponents feel “safe” or “stable” for more than 6 seconds at a time. Rhythm breaks win rounds.

Part 7: Defense Doctrine

Overview

Raven’s defense is **built on movement disruption and threat resetting**, not passive blocking. Staying unreadable defensively creates confusion.

Key Defensive Tools

  • Backdash Cancel: Evade pressure layers
  • Sidestep Right: Escape linear mid chains
  • Clone Summon: Mind trap mashers during defense
  • Teleport Reset: Break frame traps and safe mids
  • BT 1+2: Parry reversal against strings

Defense Flowchart

  • Pressure ➔ Clone Summon ➔ Teleport ➔ Punish ➔ Reset

Mindset Priority

  • Escape Early: Never get boxed at wall
  • Force Errors: Make opponent swing at clones
  • Reset Threat: Always look for mind reset opportunity

Elite Raven defense does not block for 10 seconds straight — it shifts positioning, forces mistakes, and punishes impatience rapidly.

Part 8: Clone System

Overview

Raven’s clone summons are **mind stack overload weapons** — designed to break patterns, force reactions, and bait desperation swings.

Clone Timing Layers

  • Instant Explosion: Punish mashers immediately
  • Quarter Delay: Catch steppers and delayed mashers
  • Half Delay: Crack defenders who wait too long
  • No Explosion: Bait sidesteps and fakes

Clone Usage Scenarios

  • After safe mids like D/F+1, D/B+2,1
  • Post teleport reset to overload reaction time
  • On wall splat for panic frame traps
  • During defense to fake vulnerability

Mindset Priority

  • Vary Timing: Never always explode the same way
  • Trap Movement: Bait sidestep and panic options
  • Reset Fear: Use clones to reset neutral tension instantly

Elite clone users make every summon a mind trap — clone neutral = Raven supremacy over aggressive players.

Part 9: Teleport Systems

Overview

Raven’s teleports are **advanced reset and evasion tools**, not just defensive gimmicks. Controlled teleport resets create fear, whiffs, and free punishes.

Teleport Variants

  • Back Teleport (SZN.B): Full retreat ➔ bait whiff ➔ punish
  • Up Teleport (SZN.U): Crush lows ➔ air punish options
  • Down Teleport (SZN.D): Low crush ➔ quick crouch resets

Teleport Flow Layers

  • Safe poke ➔ Back Teleport ➔ Bait swing ➔ Launch punish
  • Clone summon ➔ Up Teleport ➔ Jump cancel mind trap
  • Frame trap ➔ Down Teleport ➔ Crouch reset strike/throw mix

Mindset Priority

  • Reset Threat: Always teleport after heavy clone mindgames
  • Crush Greed: Up teleport breaks greedy low spam
  • Survive Frames: Escape death frame traps with teleport layers

Teleport is not escape — it is **battlefield dominance** via instant rhythm disruption.
