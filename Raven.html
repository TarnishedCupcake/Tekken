Stances and Transition Moves

Raven’s mastery revolves around forcing stance transitions. Only three real stances exist for Raven in Tekken 8 Season 2. Understanding when and how transitions occur is critical for executing effective pressure and mixups.

Back Turn (BT)
  • Input: Automatically entered from B+3+4 or during specific moves like BT pressure strings.
  • Purpose: Enables BT-only attacks, quick low pokes, and frame trap pressure without turning around.
Soul Zone Neutral (SZN)
  • Input: 3+4 (Clone Summon)
  • Purpose: Creates a clone in front of Raven, allowing zoning control, bait setups, and optional forced Sprint transitions via followups like SZN 1F.
Shadow Sprint (Sprint / SSP)
  • Input: QCF (Quarter Circle Forward input — Forward → Down-Forward → Down)
  • Purpose: Sprint stance gives access to Sprint attacks (QCF+1, QCF+2, QCF+1+2) and Sprint cancels for mixups and pressure loops.

Forced Transition Moves

BT 2,1F (Back Turn Sprint Transition)
  • Startup: 10F
  • On Hit: +7 → Forced Sprint
  • On Block: +3 → Forced Sprint
  • Purpose: BT extension that forcibly moves Raven into Sprint for immediate offensive flow.
SZN 1F (Soul Zone Forced Sprint Transition)
  • Startup: 14F
  • On Hit: +14 → Forced Sprint
  • On Block: +5 → Forced Sprint
  • Purpose: Clone jab poke that automatically transitions Raven into Sprint after contact, creating immediate attack pressure.

Okizeme (Knockdown Pressure)

After a knockdown, Raven excels at forcing 50/50s or setting up safe Sprint or Clone-based traps. This section outlines Raven’s true knockdown pressure tools in Tekken 8 Season 2, including tech trap paths and defensive layer coverage.

Okizeme Key Concepts

  • Immediate Sprint Cancel: Forces fast mixup timing with QCF+1 (Mid) or QCF+2 (Low).
  • Clone Summon Setup: Summon clone (3+4) on grounded opponent for fake pressure or trap setups.
  • BT Pressure Reset: After BT 2,1F Sprint transitions, re-pressure if the opponent quickstands.

Core Okizeme Setups

After Launcher or Sweep Knockdown
  • Setup: Sprint Cancel → QCF+1 / QCF+2 Feint
  • Options: Mid poke or feint backdash to bait wakeup panic attack.
After Sprint Low Knockdown (QCF+2)
  • Setup: Clone Summon → Forced mix on tech rollers
  • Options: Clone splash hit OR Sprint Cancel pressure if clone is respected.
After Wall Splat + Wall Ender
  • Setup: BT 2,1F → Sprint Cancel → QCF+1+2 (Immediate wall okizeme)
  • Options: Catch wakeup, backroll, or back tech attempts for maximum damage reset.

Okizeme Reactions Summary

  • Opponent stays grounded: Clone summon pressure.
  • Quickstands (techs instantly): Immediate Sprint attack covers wakeup frames.
  • Backrolls: Sprint Feint or Dash up QCF+1+2 catch timing.
  • Tries to attack: Sprint Cancel feint into punish trap.

Gameplan and Setups

Raven’s fundamental strategy revolves around controlling space, creating hesitation with Clone and Sprint threats, and layering attacks to mentally overload the opponent. This section covers Raven’s real neutral plan, setup paths, and tournament-proven mixup theory for Season 2.

Gameplan Structure Overview

  • Neutral Stage: Control opponent’s movement with Small Tekken pokes and clone zoning feints.
  • Midrange Stage: Sprint cancels and feint setups to create hesitation and enforce sidestep control.
  • Pressure Stage: Sprint Mid/Low mixups after BT or Clone feints to lock down defensive reactions.
  • Conversion Stage: Confirm hits into wall carry or Heat activation into Sprint enforced pressure reset.

Setup Trees

Neutral Small Tekken Setup
  • Starter: D+3 poke or F,F+2 mid check
  • Flow: Small poke hit → Clone Summon → Forced Sprint Feint OR Dash Forward Pressure
  • Purpose: Build hesitation and prep Sprint cancels.
Sprint Threat Loop
  • Starter: BT 2,1F or SZN 1F transition into Sprint
  • Flow: Sprint Mid QCF+1 / Sprint Low QCF+2~1 Cancel
  • Purpose: Mix up opponent post-Sprint with low/high traps while maintaining safe pressure.
Clone Trap Setup
  • Starter: Sprint Cancel OR whiff punish attempt
  • Flow: Clone Summon → Delay QCF+1 or wait for whiff panic response
  • Purpose: Force respect or punish reactionary movement (sidesteps, fast attacks).

Real Opponent Reaction Handling

  • Sidestepping: F+3 Homing attack or delayed clone summon trap.
  • Backdashing: WR3 or WR3+4 clone trap advance.
  • Pressuring back: Sprint Cancel feint to whiff punish with clone or safe Sprint attack.

Gameplan Flow Summary

  • Start small — control space with pokes and clone zoning.
  • Force Sprint threat — bait defensive reactions.
  • Mix Sprint Mid or Low — open defensive layers.
  • Reset into Clone or Safe Sprint Feints — maintain frame advantage control.
