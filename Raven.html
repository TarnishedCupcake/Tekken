1. Introduction

Comprehensive Raven Guide

Character Archetype
Technical mid-range mixup specialist. Excels at tempo control, clone mindgames, and positional resets.
Main Strengths
  • Strong neutral spacing with safe mids
  • Explosive clone mindgame traps
  • Powerful teleport defensive resets
  • Elite mental disruption ability
Main Weaknesses
  • Predictable clone setups if overused
  • Back Turn stance punishable if mistimed
  • Requires frequent rhythm variation to succeed
Win Conditions
Control neutral, pressure mental stack with clone setups, and reset neutral tempo through teleport threats.
Mindset Recommendation
Raven wins by disrupting mental rhythm, not overpowering. Think about stealing initiative and resetting momentum constantly.
Pro Tip: Raven isn't just punishing moves — he is punishing decisions. Own the mental rhythm.

5. Stance System & Transitions

Stance System & Transitions

Back Turn (BT)
Activated after certain strings or teleport options.
Core moves: - BT 1+2 (Reversal) - BT 3+4 (Launcher) - BT D/F+3 (Mid poke)
**Purpose:** Frame trap setups and back-facing resets.
Soulzone (SZN)
Entered with 3+4 manually or off clone summons.
Core moves: - SZN 1+2 (Unblockable explosion) - SZN D (Down teleport crush) - SZN U (Up teleport crush)
**Purpose:** Layer teleport or clone threat in neutral.
Shadow Sprint (SHS)
QCF input based sprint stance.
Core moves: - SHS 1 (Mid dash punch) - SHS 2 (Mid launcher) - SHS 3 (Low sweep)
**Purpose:** Aggressive approach options from neutral control.
Transition Philosophy
Raven’s stance transitions are about **resetting rhythm** — use Back Turn for close pressure, Soulzone for clone layering, Shadow Sprint to force aggressive counters.
Pro Tip: Always disguise stance entries behind safe pokes, clone setups, or teleports — **never raw stance switch unless baiting a reaction**.

6. Gameplan

Gameplan

Neutral Tools
Control space with 1, D/F+1, F+2. Safely prod opponents and set up clone threat.
Approach Options
Use WR2 (+ frames), F,F+3 (mid launcher) to close distance safely and with initiative.
Small Tekken/Pokes
Abuse 1, 1,1, D/B+2,1 — light poking layers to break turtling opponents slowly without committing.
Pressure + Clone Layers
Post + frames (after WR2 or clone threat), pressure safely ➔ stance entry ➔ teleport escape if blocked.
Reset Loops
Sidestep ➔ Clone Threat ➔ Teleport ➔ Reset spacing. **Force opponents to chase and overextend**.
Heat Strategy
Heat Dash from F+2,3 to extend offense. Clone summon post-Heat creates panic decisions at walls.
Pro Tip: The real Raven gameplan isn't "win neutral" — it's **reset neutral** over and over until the opponent collapses mentally.

7. Defense Doctrine

Defense Doctrine

Spacing Priority
Stay at mid-range (~2 character lengths) — Raven’s best clone threat and teleport reset range.
Backdash + Sidestep
Cycle fast backdash ➔ micro sidestep ➔ re-center. Always be ready to deny commitment.
Clone Reset Usage
Insert clone after blocking mids or eating small pokes — **forces mental reset** and buys defensive frames.
Teleport Escape
When minus, teleport up or back to crush retaliation strings. SZN.B and SZN.U should be second nature defensively.
Wall Defense
Defend with clone explosion or teleport escapes first. Never panic mash under wall pressure — **reset distance** first, punish later.
Clone Explosion Bait
Summon clone, delay explosion manually if opponent expects instant — bait panic sidesteps or presses into blast.
Pro Tip: Great Raven defense doesn't rely on blocking — it relies on **constant re-centering and forcing opponent resets**.

8. Clone System

Clone System

Instant Clone Summon
Immediate explosion.
Use when: opponent heavily presses after pokes.
Quarter Delay Clone
Brief delay before explosion.
Use when: opponent tries to sidestep or counterattack.
Half Delay Clone
Longer delay clone.
Use when: opponent expects fast explosion and freezes.
No Explosion Clone
Summon but do not detonate.
Use when: you want opponent to respect and back off automatically.
Clone Mind Trap Layers
Rotate between instant, delay, no explosion per round. Break mental stack by keeping reaction timing unstable.
Clone vs Aggressive Opponent
Summon clones defensively in neutral. Aggressive players will always overstep into explosions.
Pro Tip: If your opponent knows when the clone will explode — **you already lost the clone war**. **Rotate timings** to maintain clone threat pressure.

9. Teleport Systems

Teleport Systems

SZN.B (Soulzone Back Teleport)
Backward teleport during Soulzone stance.
Use when: opponent overcommits on approach.
Goal: Force whiffs and reset neutral spacing.
SZN.U (Soulzone Up Teleport)
Upward teleport during Soulzone stance.
Use when: opponent staggers pressure or delays strings.
Goal: Crush mid-pressure attacks and land safely outside range.
SZN.D (Soulzone Down Teleport)
Downward teleport (ground crush) during Soulzone stance.
Use when: opponent throws or grabs frequently.
Goal: Dodge command grabs and throw traps.
Teleport Trap Layers
Post-teleport clone summons or mixups.
Strategy: After resetting distance, instantly re-approach or summon clone to continue pressure cycle.
Teleport Panic Escape
If trapped at the wall, teleport instantly (SZN.B or SZN.U) to avoid block pressure collapse.
Pro Tip: **Teleport isn't just defense** — it's an **offensive reset button**. After teleport, re-center neutral and force opponents to act first.

11. Tournament Systems

Tournament Systems

Round Opening Strategy
Use safe pokes (1, D/F+1) and backdash into clone summon immediately if opponent overcommits early.
Heat Activation Plan
Heat Dash off F+2,3 to continue offense. Always try to end Heat activation with clone summon ➔ teleport trap for guaranteed mind advantage.
Time Management
After gaining life lead, cycle clone summons and teleport resets to **burn the clock**. Raven excels at winning through passive resets without chasing.
Wall Strategy
Secure fast wall splats with WR2 or Heat combos. At wall: ➔ Summon clone ➔ Threaten teleport ➔ Force hesitation ➔ Re-center if needed.
Final Round Pressure
Open with fake clone summon setups. Bait early sidesteps/mistakes to secure guaranteed wall carry combos.
Pro Tip: **Real tournament wins** with Raven come not from massive combos, but from **resetting neutral 3–5 times per round** until the opponent self-destructs.

13. Final Footer

Closing Coaching Tips

Tempo is Everything
Raven is not a brute-force character. You must control the rhythm of neutral and mental stack — never let your opponent "feel comfortable."
Clones are Threat, Not Damage
Think of clones as mental stack bombs — even without explosion damage, they freeze, delay, and manipulate opponents into worse decisions.
Teleport Reset Mindset
Every teleport is a chance to reset positioning and tempo. It's not running away — it's rebooting the match on YOUR terms.
Patience Over Combos
Raven wins tournaments with resets, small pokes, clone pressure, and critical Heat Dash moments — not risky Hail Mary combos.
Practice Reset Flow
In training mode, practice rounds where you teleport, sidestep, clone summon, and reset back to neutral **without attacking** — this develops your real Raven instincts.
Pro Tip: If Raven feels like "he needs to guess," something went wrong. Proper Raven play should always feel like the opponent is forced to guess first.
