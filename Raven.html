1. Introduction

Comprehensive Raven Guide

Character Archetype
Technical mid-range mixup specialist. Excels at tempo control, clone mindgames, and positional resets.
Main Strengths
  • Strong neutral spacing with safe mids
  • Explosive clone mindgame traps
  • Powerful teleport defensive resets
  • Elite mental disruption ability
Main Weaknesses
  • Predictable clone setups if overused
  • Back Turn stance punishable if mistimed
  • Requires frequent rhythm variation to succeed
Win Conditions
Control neutral, pressure mental stack with clone setups, and reset neutral tempo through teleport threats.
Mindset Recommendation
Raven wins by disrupting mental rhythm, not overpowering. Think about stealing initiative and resetting momentum constantly.
Pro Tip: Raven isn't just punishing moves — he is punishing decisions. Own the mental rhythm.

5. Stance System & Transitions

Stance System & Transitions

Back Turn (BT)
Activated after certain strings or teleport options.
Core moves: - BT 1+2 (Reversal) - BT 3+4 (Launcher) - BT D/F+3 (Mid poke)
Purpose: Frame trap setups and back-facing resets.
Soulzone (SZN)
Entered manually with 3+4 or off clone summons.
Core moves: - SZN 1+2 (Unblockable explosion) - SZN D (Down teleport crush) - SZN U (Up teleport crush)
Purpose: Layer teleport or clone threat pressure in neutral.
Shadow Sprint (SHS)
QCF input-based sprint stance.
Core moves: - SHS 1 (Mid dash punch) - SHS 2 (Mid launcher) - SHS 3 (Low sweep)
Purpose: Aggressive approach and burst mixup threats.
Transition Philosophy
Raven’s stance transitions are about resetting rhythm — Use Back Turn for tight pressure, Soulzone for layered threats, Shadow Sprint for quick offensive bursts.
Pro Tip: Never raw stance switch unless hidden inside pokes, clone setups, or teleport — **Stance transitions should always disguise your real intentions**.

7. Defense Doctrine

Defense Doctrine

Spacing Priority
Stay at mid-range (~2 character lengths) — Optimal range for clone setups and teleport resets.
Backdash + Sidestep Cycling
Maintain mobility at all times. Fast backdash ➔ sidestep ➔ backdash chain prevents getting trapped.
Clone Defensive Insertions
Insert clone summons mid-pressure to create "mental freezes" ➔ Opponent loses offensive momentum trying to predict explosion.
Teleport Escape Rules
When under threat: ➔ SZN.B (Back teleport) to punish overextensions ➔ SZN.U (Up teleport) to reset positioning
Wall Defense Priority
Prioritize clone summon ➔ teleport escape over mashing buttons. **Walls are won by patience and reset**, not panic.
Clone Explosion Baiting
Delay clone explosions slightly when opponent expects immediate — Catch sidestep attempts or late retaliation.
Pro Tip: **Defense isn’t passive** — Good Raven defense **manipulates the opponent’s flow**, forcing errors without taking risks.

8. Clone System

Clone System

Instant Clone Summon
Immediate explosion to catch aggressive opponents. **Use when:** You sense button mash or panic retaliation.
Quarter Delay Clone
Brief delay explosion. **Use when:** You expect sidestep attempts after clone summon.
Half Delay Clone
Extended delay explosion. **Use when:** You want opponent to freeze waiting for detonation.
No Explosion Clone
Summon clone but never explode it. **Use when:** You want mental pressure without committing.
Mind Trap Layering
Rotate between instant, delayed, and non-explosion clones throughout the match. **Goal:** Prevent opponent from mentally timing your patterns.
Clone Reset Strategy
Even if clone doesn't hit, it **resets the opponent’s offense rhythm** — This is key to Raven’s true win condition.
Pro Tip: Clones are not just "bombs" — They are **mental delay grenades** designed to freeze and disrupt opponent momentum.

9. Teleport Systems

Teleport Systems

SZN.B (Soulzone Back Teleport)
Backward teleport to dodge pressure. **Use when:** Opponent overextends or chases. **Goal:** Whiff punish after reset.
SZN.U (Soulzone Up Teleport)
Upward teleport crushes mids and repositions Raven high. **Use when:** Opponent pressures with strings. **Goal:** Escape bad wall situations cleanly.
SZN.D (Soulzone Down Teleport)
Ground teleport to dodge lows, throws, or timing traps. **Use when:** Opponent abuses low pokes or command grabs.
Teleport Trap Layers
After teleport, threaten clone summon immediately. Opponent will hesitate, giving Raven time to regain tempo.
Wall Teleport Priority
At the wall, teleport (prefer SZN.U or SZN.B) immediately — Wall pressure kills Raven if not reset.
Pro Tip: Raven teleports aren’t just escapes — **They are neutral resets** that let Raven take control without risky commitments.

11. Tournament Systems

Tournament Systems

Round Opening Strategy
Start safe with 1 or D/F+1. If opponent overcommits, summon clone early to freeze momentum.
Heat Activation Plan
Always Heat Dash after F+2,3 or clone summon traps. Focus on gaining wall pressure rather than fishing for giant combos.
Time Management
Once ahead, **stall with clone summons and teleport escapes**. Winning rounds by timeout is a valid Raven tournament strategy.
Wall Strategy
Build clone mindgames at wall. Threaten explosion to scare opponents into freezing. Reset pressure after gaining life lead.
Final Round Mindset
Play slower than usual — Force opponent to approach. Raven thrives when opponents self-destruct trying to break the clone cycles.
Pro Tip: In tournaments, "winning ugly" with clone resets and time stalls is **more powerful** than flashy risky combos.

13. Final Footer

Closing Coaching Tips

Tempo is Everything
Raven controls rounds by disrupting pace — If opponent feels "comfortable," you already lost initiative.
Clones Are Threats, Not Damage
Think of clones as mental landmines. Freezing the opponent is stronger than landing direct explosions.
Teleport Resets > Risky Defense
Always prioritize teleport escapes over fighting from bad positions. Raven wins through resets, not scrambling.
Practice Reset Loops
Drill sequences: teleport ➔ sidestep ➔ clone ➔ backdash ➔ fakeout. Build muscle memory for infinite resets under pressure.
Patience Beats Haste
Raven tournaments are won by survival resets, not rushdown kills. **Make the opponent break first.**
Pro Tip: Your true opponent isn’t just the character across the screen — **it’s their emotional stability. Break that first.**
