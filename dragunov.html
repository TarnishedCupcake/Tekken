Comprehensive Dragunov Guide

Character Archetype Overview

Dragunov is a high-pressure, momentum-based character who excels at forcing reactions with frame traps, oppressive stance transitions, and linear mid-checks. He rewards disciplined aggression and sharp defensive reads, especially when Heat is active.

Strengths

  • Outstanding rushdown and oki setups
  • Multiple plus-on-block Heat engagers
  • Safe mid pokes with strong pressure resets
  • Flexible stance routing from key strings
  • Excellent range control tools (F,F+2, WR+2)

Weaknesses

  • Linear approach — weak to sidestep left
  • Few tracking lows or safe low pokes
  • Struggles against backdash-heavy defense
  • Requires matchup-specific pressure routing

Offensive Game Plan

Dragunov excels at cycling plus frames into stance pressure or frame traps. His goal is to lock the opponent down with jabs and mids like 1,2 and D/F+1, then rotate into Heat activation (F+2,4 or WR+2) to open up low/throw mix or chase backdash with F,F+2 or WR+2 chip. At close range, SNK 1 and SNK 2 are his key loop starters.

Defensive Game Plan

Defensively, Dragunov favors spacing and baiting with B+1, sidestep right, and U+4. Use WS+1,2 and D/F+2 for punishes. Outside Heat, his panic options are limited, so defense should rely on movement and matchup-specific checks.

Playstyle Profiles

  • Aggro-Heat: Maximize chip/frame traps via WR+2 and F+2,4~Heat. Emphasizes cancel pressure and force loops.
  • Poke-Control: Use 1,2, D/F+1, and B+3 to hold range and frustrate whiff punish attempts. Use Heat for conversions, not pressure.
  • Life-Lead Turtle: Abuse movement, SS+2,3, and WR+2 spacing tools to force time-outs or mistakes. Keep Heat in reserve defensively.

Execution Tier

Dragunov is considered Mid-to-High Execution due to his stance cancel loops, Heat routing confirms, and the precision needed for movement punishes and spike okizeme traps.

Part 4: Stances & Pressure

Sneak (SNK) Overview

Sneak (SNK) is Dragunov’s offensive stance focused on frame traps, low mixups, and CH pressure. SNK is entered via specific strings that allow for seamless stance flow.

Valid SNK Transitions

  • 2,1~d/f
  • WR+1~d/f
  • F+3~d/f
  • B+4,2~d/f

Key SNK Moves

  • SNK 1: Mid, +4 on hit, -3 on block — strong pressure loop
  • SNK 2: Low knockdown, -12 on block — oki setup tool
  • SNK 3: Low sweep, +1 on hit, -13 on block — long-range low
  • SNK 1+2: Mid launcher, -14 on block — CH bait/reset

SNK Cancel & Flow Options

  • SNK~Back: Cancel to reset pressure or bait whiff
  • SNK 1 → Jab trap → d/f+1,4: Classic frame trap cycle
  • SNK 2: Risky but rewarding low rotation
  • SNK 1+2: Use as CH threat after jabs or stance entry

Pigeon Roll (PR) Overview

Pigeon Roll is Dragunov’s evasive and pressure-follow stance, commonly entered via f,f+3 or combo knockdowns. It enables unorthodox low profiling, oki chase, and timing traps.

Pigeon Roll Entry Points

  • f,f+3 (Running roll)
  • Post-knockdown from WS+1,3 and certain Heat enders

Key Pigeon Roll Moves

  • PR 1: Mid shoulder, +2 on hit, -9 on block — oki pressure
  • PR 2: Low sweep, knockdown, -13 on block — high risk/oki reward
  • PR 1+2: Launcher on clean hit, -14 on block — late tech trap
  • PR Cancel: Cancel stance mid-roll to reset or bait

Stance Pressure Flow

+ Frames → SNK Transition → Mid/Low/CH Mix → Wall or Heat Loop

Hit Confirm Tree

2,1~d/f → SNK 1 → On hit → Jab confirm → d/f+1,4 or WR+2 Heat
