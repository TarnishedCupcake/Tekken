Stance System & Transitions

Steve’s stance system defines his pressure, movement, and mixups. Here’s a breakdown of each:

StanceInputPropertiesTransitionsStrengths
Flicker (FLK) Auto after many attacks (e.g., B+1, D/F+2,1) Mid-range pressure, jab traps → FLK 1/2/1+2, → Duck, → Albatross Safe pokes, CH pressure, jab confirms
Peekaboo (PAB) Back+1+2 Plus on block strings, baiting stance → PAB 1+2, PAB F+2, → Duck Stagger + mixup, defensive pressure, wall threat
Duck D, N (neutral crouch) Launcher access, evasion tool → WS 1,2 / WS 2 / F+2,1+2 Whiff punishing, anti-high, surprise lows
Sway B (after certain attacks or PAB) Backdash cancel, auto-evade → Sway B+1, → Flicker, → Cancel Evade + whiff trap setups
Albatross F (while in FLK) Movement reset, stance mix → Albatross 1/2/1+2, → FLK Mobile stance switch, Heat re-engage

Mastering transitions between stances and recognizing cancel options is key to Steve’s pressure and bait game. Use FLK to initiate and PAB or Duck to finish.

Combos

These combos are categorized by execution level and scenario type. Practice each set to ensure consistency in match play.

  • Easy: B+1 → D/F+2,1 → FLK 1 → F+2,1+2 (54 dmg)
  • Optimized: B+1 → Dash D/F+2,1 → FLK 1 → D/F+1 → Sonic Fang (62 dmg)
  • Wall Carry: B+1 → Dash D/F+2,1 → FLK 1 → D/F+1, Sonic Fang → Wall Pressure (65 dmg)
  • Heat Combo: B+1 → Heat Dash → F+2,1+2 → FLK Cancel → B+1 → Sonic Fang (70+ dmg)
  • Floor Break Combo: D/B+2 → F+2,1+2 → Down 1 → FLK 1+2 (breaks floor, 64 dmg)
  • Wall Combo: S! → FLK 1+2 → F+2,1+2 (Wall Splat Ender, 78 dmg)
  • Reset Combo: B+1 → D/F+2,1 → Delay FLK 1 → Duck Cancel → Throw or Low
  • Execution Heavy: Sway Cancel → Instant FLK 1+2 → F+2,1+2 → Wall Carry (high difficulty, 75+ dmg)

Always route based on stage position, Heat status, and mental stack overload setups.

Mental Stack Engineering

Steve excels at overloading mental stack through speed, delay, and CH traps. Use these principles to force defensive collapse:

  • Jab Check Stack: 1 → Delay → D/F+1 → FLK 1 → Mid / Low Layer
  • FLK Pressure Stack: FLK 1 → FLK 1+2 → Delay FLK 1 → Duck Cancel → B+1 CH fish
  • Heat Trigger Stack: Heat Dash → F+2,1+2 → FLK Cancel → Bait jab → Launcher
  • Backdash Layering: Backdash after -3 to bait CH → B+1 or FLK 1+2

Rotate stack layers based on opponent reactions. Always track their response to each mental load and flip the layer on their adaptation.

Mental Stack & Pressure Engineering

Steve thrives on overwhelming the opponent’s mental stack. Layer your offense with safe checks, CH threats, and Heat pressure to force errors.

  • Layer 1 – Jab Check: Use 1, D/F+1 or FLK 1 to open the round or reset neutral. Frame trap with B+1.
  • Layer 2 – Flicker Cancel Trap: FLK 1 → Cancel → Delay D/F+1 or throw. If they mash → B+1 for CH.
  • Layer 3 – Heat Overload: Activate Heat → F+2,1+2 → Sway → FLK 1 or CH trap. Stack mid/low/throw to overload reactions.
  • Layer 4 – Defensive Crack: Use Sway cancel → Backdash → FLK 1 → Jab bait → CH punish

Master these flow paths to manage tempo and trigger defensive panic in high-stakes play.

Matchups

  • Jin: Step left 2,4. Parry or fuzzy 1,2,4. Punish F+2. Respect parry timing.
  • Kazuya: Avoid electric by stepping left, sway cancel baits. Don’t mash vs D/F+1 pressure.
  • Leroy: Delay jab strings to break sabaki rhythm. Punish D/F+1+2. Duck hermit lows.
  • Paul: Bait Death Fist. Step right QCF+2. Don’t overpoke after QCF+1.
  • Law: Step left DSS 4. Interrupt Dragon Hammer pressure. Low parry slide.
  • Reina: Sidestep left heavy. Use B+1 to challenge stance cancels. Watch for unblockables.
  • Feng: Use FLK pressure to beat sabakis. Punish 1+2. Sway Duck vs shoulder follow-ups.
  • Zafina: Stay midrange. Use Sonic Fang to reset. Backdash low string traps.
  • Claudio: Respect hopkick range. Force whiffs. Use D/F+1 jabs after F+2+3 charge.
  • Raven: Stay grounded. Step and punish teleport entries. Use jab pressure post B+2 strings.

Okizeme & Wall/Floor Tech

  • Wakeup Trap 1: B+1 knockdown → Delay FLK 1 → D/F+1/throw mix
  • Wakeup Trap 2: D/B+1 → Heat Dash → Backdash → FLK 1 → Mid or Low
  • Floor Break Route: D/B+2 → F+2,1+2 → Down jab → FLK 1+2 (Floor Break)
  • Wall Splat Setup: Dash B+1 → D/F+2,1 → FLK 1+2 (Wall Splat)
  • Wall Ender: F+2,1+2 → FLK Cancel → PAB 1+2 (max corner pressure)
  • Ground Control: D+1 or D/B+1 post wall combo to force crouch on wakeup

Use these sequences to extend pressure after knockdowns and capitalize on wall/floor stage features.

Anti-Steve Tech

  • Backdash > Low: Steve’s FLK cancels can whiff vs backdashers. Backdash → low poke interrupts FLK cancel bait.
  • Sabakis & Parries: Characters like Leroy or Jun can sabaki FLK 1 and D/F+1 strings. Use heat cancel to bait.
  • Interrupting PAB: Use fast mids (i13–14) to beat Peekaboo stance entry. Duck → mid or step → punish.
  • Sidestepping: Step left often avoids B+1 and Sonic Fang if improperly spaced.
  • Throw Reactions: Steve has average throw game. Breaking throw attempts is a common counter in reset sequences.
  • Heat Trap Defense: After Heat Dash, delay tech or fuzzy guard to avoid FLK 1 → CH → launcher layers.

Understanding how opponents beat Steve will help you layer in safe cancels, bait timing, and stance transitions better.

Controller Tips (Pad vs Stick)

Pad Players

  • Recommended Mapping: R1 = 1+2, R2 = Heat Burst, L1 = 3+4 (easy stance access)
  • Movement Tip: Use directional buttons for clean sway and sidesteps. Avoid accidental diagonals.
  • FLK Cancel Optimization: Assign FLK stance to shortcut button if supported (e.g., via macro).

Stick Players

  • Mapping: Standard 4-button layout is optimal. Use 5th button (top row) for 1+2 macro.
  • Heat Execution: Practice Heat Dash cancel with joystick sweep → FLK cancel for timing precision.
  • Weave Practice: Stick movement aids in canceling PAB → Duck → Sway more consistently than pad.

Choose based on what allows better heat cancels, FLK cancel pressure, and execution comfort in long sets.
