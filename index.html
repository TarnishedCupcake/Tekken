4. Stance System & Transitions

Steve’s stance control forms the core of his offensive depth. Below is a breakdown of each stance, how to enter, cancel, and transition between them, plus their strategic usage.

Peekaboo (PAB)

  • Input: F+3+4
  • Enters From: F+2,1F; F+3+4 raw; FLK cancel
  • Transitions: B (Sway), 1+2 (throw), 1/2/1+2 attacks, DCK (F+3/F+4)
  • Use: Mid/throw mixups, wall pressure, Heat trap extensions

Flicker (FLK)

  • Input: B+3+4
  • Enters From: 1 (delay), D/F+2,1, B+1 on block, manual B+3+4
  • Transitions: F (Albatross), B (Sway), cancels into pressure tools
  • Use: Frame traps, jab checks, stance cancels

Ducking (DCK)

  • Input: F+3 or F+4
  • From Stance: PAB → F+3~F, F+4~F
  • Use: Access to WS attacks, evasion of highs, CH bait

Sway (SWY)

  • Input: B+3 or B+4 (from FLK or PAB)
  • Use: Evasion, CH baiting, cancels back to neutral

Left Weave (LWV)

  • Input: 3
  • Use: Linear mid evade, sidestep to left, FLK/CH trap setup

Right Weave (RWV)

  • Input: 4
  • Use: Evasion to Steve’s right, reset mixups

Albatross (ALB)

  • Input: 3+4
  • Use: Movement stance, aerial mixups, rotate positioning
  • From: Raw input or FLK F transition

Lionheart

  • Input: U/B+3
  • Use: Unique evasion stance, anti-linear tool, trap setups

6. Gameplan and Setups

This section merges neutral strategy, poke theory, pressure setups, flowcharts, and oki tactics into a cohesive tournament-ready system.

Neutral Tools

  • D/F+1: Mid poke, safe at -1
  • 1 (Jab): CH check, FLK entry
  • B+1: CH bait, wall carry starter
  • FLK 1: Whiff punisher with stance access
  • Backdash → F,F+2: Whiff trap

Keep-Out Moves

  • F,F+2: Long-range safe poke
  • D/B+2: Defensive low, slow but knocks down
  • 1+2: Wall splat from range

Pokes & Small Tekken

  • 2,1: Safe mid follow-up
  • D+1: Low chip, safe on block
  • F+3: Duck entry check + plus frames
  • Micro backdash → jab: Space control

Pressure & Mix-Ups

  • FLK 1 → delay FLK 2: Frame trap
  • PAB 1 → DCK 1+2: Mid/mid pressure
  • PAB → Throw vs PAB → DCK: Mix setup
  • Sway cancel → jab reset: Movement trap

Setups & Frame Traps

  • CH B+1 → FLK 2: Guaranteed launcher
  • F+2,1F → delay PAB 1+2: Throw or CH trap
  • FLK 1 → DCK cancel → low poke: Delay test

Okizeme

  • D/B+2 → FLK: Pressure on wakeup
  • DCK 1+2: Re-hit tech roll options
  • W! → Step → 2,1 → FLK cancel: Fake reset

Flowcharts

  • FLK 1 → FLK 2 → PAB 1 → Throw or DCK: Mid/throw/low loop
  • PAB 1 → DCK 1 → Delay 1+2: Trap CH path
  • CH B+1 → FLK 2 → PAB cancel → Delay throw: Full loop

Round Starters

  • D/F+1, Jab, or B+1: Safe openers
  • Backdash → F,F+2: Bait opening

Round Closers

  • D+1: Safe chip finisher
  • FLK 1 → delay FLK 1+2: Closeout vs mash
  • PAB → throw: Mental crush when low

Timer Strategy

  • 40s left: Maintain wall advantage
  • 20s left: Start stacking CH traps
  • 10s left: Delay DCK 1 or force time-out lead

7. FoxStack Curriculum (8-Week Training)

This is a structured 8-week training program built for players preparing for tournaments. It includes movement practice, punish drills, combo labbing, Heat setups, and matchup testing.

Week 1: Core Fundamentals

  • Backdash cancel → jab drill
  • FLK 1,2 hit confirms 100x
  • i10–i14 punisher lab session

Week 2: Movement and Whiff Punishing

  • Backdash → F,F+2 vs standing dummies
  • Sidestep → B+1 or WS 1,2 50x per side
  • Wall carry combo → wall reset drill

Week 3: CH Setups and Pressure

  • FLK 1/FLK 2/throw pressure simulation
  • B+1 → FLK 1 reaction launcher test
  • PAB → Throw vs DCK bait mixup lab

Week 4: Heat Route Execution

  • CH B+1 → Heat Dash routes
  • Wall carry → Heat extension check
  • Combo timer test (vs 10s/20s left)

Week 5: Matchup Replay Review

  • Review 5 losing replays → identify stack traps
  • Make 10-character flashcards with punish strings
  • Practice vs known Steve counters

Week 6: Wall Strategy Focus

  • Wall pressure loop (FLK cancel → jab reset)
  • Fake reset vs tech trap 50x
  • W! → Step → FLK 1/FLK 1+2 branch

Week 7: Clutch Defense + Heat Mind Games

  • Delay traps → fuzzy guard test
  • Panic baiting drills
  • Practice full Heat route TODs from CH launcher

Week 8: Bracket Sim + Tournament Mental

  • Play 20 ranked matches no rage quit
  • Review opponent Heat options & Steve anti-tech
  • Checklist: TO readiness, water/notes/review

10. Anti-Steve Tech

Understand how players counter Steve’s gameplan and how to beat their adaptations. This section exposes known anti-Steve tactics and how to reverse-engineer their responses.

Common Counters

  • High Crush: Duck vs FLK 1 → 2
  • Sidewalk Right: Evades FLK 1+2 and sway cancel
  • Low Parry: D+1 and PAB cancel lows
  • Throw Tech: PAB → 1+2 throw becomes obvious when overused

Sabaki/Parry Weakness

  • Leroy’s Hermit parry punishes FLK pressure
  • Jun parries PAB 1 or FLK 2
  • Generic low parry kills Duck cancel strings

Stance Break Chart

StanceCounter OptionHow to Beat It

PABJab interruptDelay → CH B+1
DCKLow parryCancel → FLK 1+2 trap
SwayBackdash punishFake cancel → jab reset

Reversal Strategy

  • If they sidewalk FLK: Cancel → DCK 1+2 or jab trap
  • If they duck PAB: Cancel into mid or throw delay
  • If they fuzzy: Delay trap → Heat Dash setup
  • If they parry: Bait with D+1 → jab → B+1

11. Controller-Specific Tips

This section includes button mapping advice, stance input tricks, and optimization tips for both pad and stick users to maximize execution.

Recommended Pad Layout

  • L1: 1+2 (Throw)
  • R1: 3+4 (Albatross / Stance cancel)
  • L2: DCK 1+2 macro (or optional Heat)
  • R2: Heat Burst (if preferred)

Recommended Stick Layout

  • Top Row: 1 / 2 / 3
  • Bottom Row: 4 / 1+2 / 3+4
  • Bind Heat or DCK 1+2 to pinky button or side trigger

Execution Tips

  • Use input buffer to slide into PAB/Flicker
  • Practice FLK cancel → DCK 1+2 50x clean reps
  • Train flicker cancel jab with plinked 1~B+3+4 input

Common Errors

  • Missing FLK to DCK due to improper neutral reset
  • Overcommitting to 2,1 strings instead of confirming
  • Inputting 1+2 throw too slowly in PAB

11. Controller-Specific Tips

Whether you use pad or stick, optimizing your controller layout is essential for fast stance cancels, Heat inputs, and consistent movement.

Pad Users

  • Recommended Layout: Bind 1+2 and 3+4 to shoulder buttons
  • Use left thumb for D-Pad movement, right thumb for attacks
  • Bind Heat Burst to L2 or R2 for fast activation from stance
  • Hold PAB stance via shoulder while pressing directional strings

Stick Users

  • Recommended Layout: 1/2/3/4 + Heat Burst button mapped left of 1
  • Use wrist roll for fast 3+4 (Albatross) and DCK cancels
  • Option select PAB throw via quick 1+2 button
  • Use bottom buttons for smooth Duck → DCK → FLK cancels

Execution Practice

  • Drill: FLK 1 → DCK 1 → Heat Dash → combo (x50 reps)
  • Drill: PAB 1 → DCK cancel → FLK 2 → trap
  • Drill: Sway → jab reset → B+1 confirm
